Michael Jordan

Fun Facts about Michael Jordan's interesting life

  • Michael Jordan was famously cut from his high school varsity basketball team as a sophomore.
  • He wore the number 23 because it was half of his brother’s number, 45.
  • Jordan won six NBA championships and six Finals MVPs—never losing a Finals series.
  • He briefly retired from basketball in 1993 to play professional baseball.
  • Jordan scored 63 points in a single playoff game against the Celtics, an NBA record.
  • He averaged 30.1 points per game for his career—the highest in NBA history.
  • His Air Jordan sneaker line helped turn athletes into global fashion icons.
  • Jordan won five regular-season MVP awards during his NBA career.
  • He is the only player to win NBA MVP and Defensive Player of the Year in the same season (1988).
  • Michael Jordan became the first billionaire NBA player, largely due to endorsements.
